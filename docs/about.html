About

About Genesis Lodge

A supportive, family-style home for adult men living with mental health challenges, located in a restored heritage property in downtown Brampton.

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide a safe, dignified home and a structured environment where men living with mental health challenges can maintain stability, develop daily living skills, and stay connected to their community.

We believe that every person deserves respect, privacy, and the opportunity to participate in making decisions about their life and care.

Our History

Genesis Lodge operates from a historic property on Church Street East, originally built in the 18th century and known locally for its distinctive architecture and long-standing presence in the community.

Over time, the home has transitioned to a supportive housing setting, while maintaining the warmth and character of a family home.

Heritage Home

The building has served as a residence in Brampton for decades, with many original architectural features preserved.

Focus On Mental Health

Genesis Lodge became a supportive home for men with mental illness, offering structured routines and coordinated supports.

Ongoing Evolution

We continue to adapt our programs, supports, and partnerships based on resident needs, community resources, and best practices in mental health support.

Our Approach

Person-Centred

We recognize each resident as an individual with their own history, strengths, challenges, and preferences. Supports are tailored rather than one-size-fits-all.

Strengths-based

We focus on what residents can do, and help build on those strengths to support daily functioning and engagement with meaningful activities.

Collaborative

We work with families, case workers, physicians, clinics, hospitals, and community programs to coordinate care and support continuity.

Our Team

Genesis Lodge is supported by a small, dedicated team of staff and community partners. While we do not publish a full staff directory online, residents and families can expect support from:

  • Support workers providing day-to-day assistance and supervision
  • A Registered Nurse supporting health monitoring and medication administration
  • Access to a family physician and other health-care providers
  • Administrative and coordination staff overseeing admissions and care planning

Families are encouraged to stay involved in planning and to contact us with questions or concerns at any time.
