Our Services

Genesis Lodge provides day-to-day support in a structured, stable environment to help residents manage their lives and mental health more confidently.

What we provide

Residents have access to a range of supports, including:

  • A supportive, structured living environment
  • Help coordinating with a family physician and referrals as needed
  • Medication administration and monitoring
  • Registered nursing support
  • Coordination with pharmacy services
  • Visits from a certified foot care nurse by appointment
  • Laundry and housekeeping services
  • Nutritious, well-balanced meals

At a glance

  • Structured daily routines
  • Health and medication support
  • On-site meals and housekeeping
  • Links to community programs

Accommodation

Genesis Lodge offers shared and, when available, private rooms in a fully furnished home-like setting.

Semi-private rooms

Comfortable, fully furnished rooms shared with another resident, designed to feel like home rather than an institution.

Private rooms (when available)

A limited number of private rooms may be available depending on current occupancy. Availability can be discussed during intake.

Rates

Rates are based on each person’s income and funding situation, with flexibility to discuss individual circumstances during inquiry.

We work with residents who receive fixed monthly income through programs such as the Ontario Disability Support Program (ODSP), Ontario Works (OW), and the Canada Pension Plan (CPP).

If someone has a different financial situation, we encourage them to contact us so that details can be reviewed and clarified at the time of inquiry.

Questions about rates?

Our intake team can walk through current rates and available funding options based on your situation.

Activities & community life

A funded activity program helps residents stay active, connected, and engaged with the community around them.

Activities are designed to encourage social interaction, independence, and the development of friendships. Residents are supported to build social and life skills through involvement in community-based programs and groups.

Programming may include connections to community organizations, day treatment and rehabilitation programs, as well as outings to recreational events, entertainment, sports, and local restaurants.

Program goals

  • Promote a sense of belonging
  • Encourage independence and self-worth
  • Build social and life skills
  • Support participation in community life
